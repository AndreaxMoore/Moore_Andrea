Steps to make Chinese dumplings

  1. Prepare the wrappers
  2. Make the fillings
  3. Scoop the filling onto each wrapper
  4. Fold the sides of the wrapper together
  5. Squeeze the wrapper carefully
  6. Put the dumplings in boiling water
  7. Transfer the dumplings onto a plate

XHTML&CSS

XHTML & CSS: code standards that make websites work.
Text files: the most basic level of websites.

Tags

Every component in a website is contained within a tag. The first tag you create on a website declares the doctype.

Scope

Everything in a web object is contained within the opening and closing tags.

Head

"< head >< / head >"
Includes information such as the keywords and the title for the browser. Not seen by the users.

Body

Includes all of the actual site content, such as paragraphs, images, audio, animations, backgrounds.

Paragraphs:
The tag allows us to tell the browser explicitly which text we want to put into paragraph form.
Inline Text Tags, line break:
Tag for adding a single space inside of a paragraph tag.
Horizontal Rule:
Adds a horizontal line, acts as a separator.
Strong tag:
Puts the enclosed text in bold.
Em tag:
Italicizes the enclosed text.

Anchor Tag

Allows us to link to other sites, other pages on our own site, or specific places in a particular page.

Other

Header:
A system of section titles arranged from h1 to h6 in descending order. h1 has the biggest font size, h6 has the smallest.
Adding a Page Navigation With Links:
Add links to separate parts of pages by naming different sections and creating links to them.
Tool Tip:
More information about the link.
Adding Images to Your Site:
Adjust the height attribute to adjust the size of the image.
